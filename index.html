Te-Yen Wu

A PhD candidate from XDiscovery Lab at Dartmouth College, advised by Xing-Dong Yang. His research vision is to allow devices to be instead easily created from smart everyday materials at large scale using established methods, by which everyday objects is created Specifically, his PhD research focuses on human-material interactions, which investigates the compelling interactions for smart materials and the sensing technologies enabling the interactions on smart materials. Besides, he has also worked on other topics in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), such as AR/VR interactions, Accessible Interface and Educational Tool. He has a strong record of 15/17 papers published at top HCI venues (CHI, and UIST). This includes a Best paper award at UIST 19 and an Honorable Mention award at CHI 2020. Further, my research has also attracted numerous public interests via Internet News (e.g. Engadget, Times).
